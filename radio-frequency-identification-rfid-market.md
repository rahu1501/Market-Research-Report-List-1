<p><p>Radio-Frequency Identification (RFID) is a technology that uses radio waves to automatically identify and track objects or people. It consists of a tag or label that contains a unique identifier and an antenna, and a reader or scanner that emits radio waves to communicate with the tag and retrieve the information stored in it. RFID has various applications across different industries, including supply chain management, retail, healthcare, transportation, and agriculture.</p><p>The future outlook for the RFID market is promising. The need for efficient supply chain management, improved inventory visibility, and enhanced security measures are driving the demand for RFID solutions.</p><p>Additionally, advancements in RFID technology, such as the development of smaller and more cost-effective tags, improved read ranges, and integration with other technologies like IoT, are further fueling the market growth.
