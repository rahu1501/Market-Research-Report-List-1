<p><p>Wave soldering fluxes are used in the electronics industry to clean and protect metal surfaces during the wave soldering process. These fluxes can be divided into two main types: corrosive and noncorrosive. Corrosive fluxes are designed to remove oxides from metal surfaces, allowing for better solderability. However, they can also be aggressive and may cause corrosion if not properly cleaned after soldering. Noncorrosive fluxes, on the other hand, are milder and do not cause corrosion. They are suitable for sensitive electronic components that are more prone to damage. Both types have their own advantages and are chosen based on specific soldering requirements.</p></p>
