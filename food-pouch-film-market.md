<p><p>The food pouch film market comprises various types of films used for packaging food products. Aluminum foil is a popular choice for its barrier properties, protecting food against moisture, oxygen, and light. BOPP (biaxially oriented polypropylene) film offers excellent clarity and resistance to moisture, making it suitable for snacks and confectionery. BOPA (biaxially oriented polyamide) film provides high puncture resistance and gas barrier properties, often used for meat and cheese packaging. BOPET (biaxially oriented polyester) film offers excellent tensile strength and heat resistance, ideal for hot-fill applications. CPP (cast polypropylene) film provides enhanced sealability and moisture resistance. LLDPE (linear low-density polyethylene) film is flexible and impact-resistant, commonly used for manufacturing stand-up pouches. Other types of films, depending on specific requirements, may include nylon, EVOH (ethylene vinyl alcohol), or laminated films.</p></p>
