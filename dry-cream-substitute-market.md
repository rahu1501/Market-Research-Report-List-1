<p><p>The dry cream substitute market consists of two main types: powder and liquid. Powdered dry cream substitutes are made by dehydrating cream and converting it into a fine powder form. This type is convenient for longer shelf life, easy transportation, and storage. Liquid dry cream substitutes are produced by emulsifying fats and non-dairy ingredients into a liquid form, providing a convenient option for immediate use and easy mixing. Both types provide alternatives for those who cannot or choose not to consume dairy cream while maintaining similar functionalities and taste.</p></p>
